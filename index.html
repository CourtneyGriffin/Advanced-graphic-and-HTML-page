The Critical Link Between Race, Ethnicity and Black Infant Mortality in America

Exploring the Impact of Systemic Inequities on Health Outcomes for Black Infants: The Effects of Redlining and Racial Bias on the Black Community

by Courtney Griffin
November 25, 2024

Black infants experience significantly higher mortality rates than their white and Asian counterparts, underscoring urgent systemic issues in healthcare access and social determinants of health nationwide. This disparity highlights the need for systemic reform in healthcare, linking to broader discussions on racial equity and social justice.




According to Michele Akosua Chin-On, a long-time doula and private duty nurse, redlining not only impacts a community's finances but also disrupts its cellular structure due to inadequate access to quality healthcare and racial bias, among other factors. This leads to increased stress and cortisol levels in Black mothers, which directly affects the health of their infants.

“These inequalities are like a visceral pain,” said Chin-On.” It's proof that Black women are not heard, taken care of and seen, which hurts their infants. If you nurture a mother, you nurture a nation and these charts clearly show America is not doing that.”
